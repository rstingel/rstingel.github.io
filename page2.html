Carbon Footprint

What is carbon footprint?

Carbon footprint corresponds to the whole amount of greenhouse gasses (GHG) produced to, directly and indirectly, support a person's lifestyle and activities.

How to reduce it in the community?

We can cut urban sprawl as much as possible. Next, we can reduce waste and divert it away from landfills. Speaking of renewable energy, we need to produce more of it. Also, we can establish a convenient public transit system. Lastly, we can all be green at home.

How to reduce it personally?

You must consume local and seasonal products (forget strawberries in water). You can limit meat consumption, especially beef. Easy enough, select fish from sustainable fishing. Also, make sure to take care of your clothes. Be smart when you're driving; idling the car will make things worse. For your next holiday, take the train. Thus, this excerpt indicates how to reduce carbon footprint personally.

What are its effects?

Usually, the majority of an individual's carbon footprint will come from transportation, housing and food. The effects of this will usually have bad consequences. For example, if we continue to do this, the ozone layer of the earth's atmosphere, will disapear. The ozone layer has protected us from the sun's deadly radiation. If the ozone layer did not exist, we wouldn't be here right now.

Why is carbon footprint important?

Carbon footprint is important because you need to understand the impact of an individual's behavior on global warming.
